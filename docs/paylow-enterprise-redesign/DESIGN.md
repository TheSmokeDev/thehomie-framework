## Overview

Pay Low Enterprise Trust is built to make a local insurance agency look credible enough for modern buyers and valuable enough for a business owner to pay for the rebuild. The design should feel like a regional financial-services brand, not a coupon flyer.

The site must communicate three things in the first viewport: Pay Low is local, Pay Low can quote multiple insurance products, and Pay Low can handle the annoying DMV/service paperwork customers want done fast.

## Colors

The palette keeps Pay Low's recognizable blue, red, and orange equity but professionalizes the usage. Navy owns trust and structure. Orange is reserved for quote actions. Red and green are used sparingly for product families and status accents.

Do not flood the page with flat royal blue. Use white, sky, and paper surfaces to create expensive-looking contrast.

## Typography

Use strong, tight Inter headlines with high contrast and clear hierarchy. Body text should stay readable and operational. Avoid oversized mobile paragraphs; buyers should scan the offer quickly.

## Layout

The first viewport uses an enterprise split: left side value proposition, right side actionable quote card, with local proof and service chips visible. Below the hero, use full-width bands for trust, services, locations, and an AI receptionist/productivity wedge.

Cards are allowed for repeated items only: service tiles, locations, testimonials, and quote form modules.

## Elevation & Depth

Use soft shadows and subtle borders. The design should look built, not decorated. Avoid floating blobs, heavy gradients, or fake glass effects.

## Shapes

Corners stay at 6-12px. Insurance is a trust category; do not use childish pill-heavy styling except for compact service tags.

## Components

Primary CTAs are orange with white text. Secondary CTAs are white or transparent with navy borders. Form fields are large, simple, and mobile-safe. Every quote path should feel like a service desk, not a generic lead form.

## Do's and Don'ts

Do lead with local trust, speed, bilingual service, and proof.

Do show the three locations above the fold or immediately after.

Do preserve Pay Low's recognizable brand colors while making the page feel expensive.

Do not use the current site's giant empty logo band, mismatched icon grids, stretched stock cards, or tiny desktop nav on mobile.

Do not claim exact savings or carrier access unless Pay Low verifies it.
